## Hannah Arendt's definition 
![[Pasted image 20260611192744.png|212]]
A specifically **modern, secular[^1] nineteenth-century ideology** that must be clearly distinguished from traditional **religious Jew-hatred**. While religious hatred was inspired by the antagonism[^2] between conflicting creeds[^3], modern antisemitism is a **secular racist ideology** that emerged in the 1870s.

> [!info]
> - **Political vs. Religious:** Antisemitism became a political weapon and an **ideological catalyst** for totalitarianism because of the unique sociological position of the Jewish people—<mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">stateless yet wealthy—within the emerging European nation-states.</mark>
> - **The Paradox of Wealth without Power:** Arendt argues that antisemitism reached its climax when Jews lost their public functions and influence (power) but retained their wealth. She posits that <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">**wealth without visible function** is more intolerable to the masses than actual oppression, leading to the perception of Jews as parasites.</mark>
> - **Relationship to the Nation-State:** Modern antisemitism grew as traditional nationalism declined, reaching its peak exactly when the European nation-state system crashed. <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">Jews were the only "inter-European element"[^8] in a world of growing nationalization, making them a target for movements seeking to destroy the nation-state.</mark>
> - **"Vice" vs. "Crime":** Arendt explains the <mark style="background: #ABF7F7A6;">psychological shift</mark>[^9] enabling modern antisemitism through the distinction between a **crime** (a specific punishable act) and a **vice** (a crime accepted as an inherent, racial trait of a group). Under this framework, <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">being Jewish was no longer a matter of[^7] faith or action but a **"racial stain"** or predestination</mark>[^4] from which there was no escape through conversion.
> - **Catalytic Agent for Totalitarianism:** In the Nazi movement, antisemitism served as the **"amalgamator"**[^5] or catalytic agent around which other elements of totalitarianism crystallized[^6].<mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;"> It provided a "fictitious world conspiracy"</mark> (such as the forged _Protocols of the Elders of Zion_) that proved more attractive to the masses than traditional economic or political arguments.

Arendt sees antisemitism <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">not as a historical accident, but as a **modern political phenomenon** shaped by the collapse of traditional nation-states, the secularization of society, and the loss of the Jews' historical social functions.</mark>
